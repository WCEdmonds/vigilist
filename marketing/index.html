Big-firm tools, small-firm reality.

Traditional e-discovery platforms are built — and priced — for hundred-lawyer firms, and even then the workflow is stitched together from separate products for review, redaction, production, and analytics. Vigilist is the whole lifecycle in one place: everything a lean litigation team needs to take a matter from load file to defensible production, and nothing you don't.

What's inside

The whole matter lifecycle. One platform.

Ingest anything, keep adding

Relativity-style DAT/OPT productions with AI-assisted field mapping, PST/EML email containers with families and threads intact, or folders of PDFs and natives. Matters take multiple loads — their productions and your collection, side by side and clearly labeled.

AI review that shows its work

An LLM reads every document against your case criteria and codes it — relevant, key, or not — with confidence scores, reasoning, and excerpts. Attorneys stay in command with one-click overrides.

Validated like the courts expect

Blind control sets, recall and precision with confidence intervals, and elusion testing over the discard pile — the TAR validation statistics that turn "we used AI" into a number a judge accepts.

Search every way

Full-text with phrases, booleans, and wildcards; semantic search when you don't know the words; and search-term hit reports — hits, family-expanded, and unique counts — for negotiating term lists with evidence.

A brief that writes itself

Clustering, theme labels, and an AI case brief with citations that jump straight to the Bates number — your first read of a new production, done before you sit down.

Redactions that can't leak

Draw redactions with reason codes and burn them into every produced pixel. Second-pass QC approvals auto-invalidate if a redaction changes afterward — stale sign-offs structurally cannot ship.

Privilege logs, generated

Flag privilege tags and Vigilist derives withhold and redact-in-part dispositions, then builds the privilege log from safe metadata only — never from privileged content. Export to CSV, ready to serve.

Produce like the incumbents

Build a production set, lock it for gap-free Bates numbers with families kept together, stamp and render to PDF or classic single-page TIFF, carve out spreadsheets as natives, split volumes by size — and deliver Concordance DAT/OPT load files any platform can ingest.

Guardrails before the gavel

Locking a production runs validation first: unapproved redactions, privileged documents about to go out clean, received documents headed back to their producer. Conflicts must be resolved — or overridden on the record, with a name attached.

Chain of custody, on demand

Every document tells its story: source, hashes, extraction, tags, review decisions, redactions, and the productions it went out in. Matter-level processing reports and an honest exceptions ledger, one click away.

Built for review speed

Keyboard-driven coding, bulk tagging across thousands of documents, review queues and batches with statistical QC, and an in-browser viewer for page images, text, natives, and media.

Enterprise-ready security

Single sign-on with your firm's identity provider (SAML/OIDC) — enforceable per organization — role-based access, and an append-only audit trail behind every action in the platform.

Entity intelligence

Know who's who before the first deposition.

Vigilist reads the corpus and builds the cast of characters: every person and organization, resolved across their aliases — "J. Rivera," "Rivera, Jorge," and the email address are one person, with a review queue for the close calls, so a human confirms every merge.

Click any name, anywhere, and get the profile: an AI-written overview, every mention with its surrounding context, who they're connected to and how — employer, counsel, correspondent, party to an agreement — each relationship backed by the phrase in the document that proves it. The key-players view ranks the matter's cast by presence, so the people who matter surface on day one, not week three.

Events — meetings, payments, agreements — are extracted from day one too, building toward matter timelines and relationship graphs on the same foundation.

How it works

From load file to defensible production.

  1. 1

    Ingest

    Drop in their production or your client's mailboxes. Metadata, families, threads, hashes, and OCR are handled automatically — and you can keep adding loads to the same matter as the case grows.

  2. 2

    Review with AI

    The AI codes the corpus and briefs your team; reviewers work the queues with keyboard-speed tagging. Validate the machine with control sets and elusion tests whenever the numbers need to hold up.

  3. 3

    Redact & log privilege

    Draw redactions, QC them, and let the privilege log build itself from your privilege calls — dispositions, descriptions, and all.

  4. 4

    Produce & defend

    Lock the set, stamp Bates, render, and package DAT/OPT with a hashed manifest. Hit reports, sampling statistics, and custody reports stand behind every choice you made.

Defensible by design.

Every claim Vigilist helps you make comes with its evidence attached. Redaction approvals that expire when the redaction changes. Production locks that record who overrode which warning. Frozen random samples with Wilson confidence intervals. Validation reports that persist exactly as they were run. An append-only audit log under all of it. When opposing counsel asks "how do you know?" — you'll have the number, the interval, and the record.
